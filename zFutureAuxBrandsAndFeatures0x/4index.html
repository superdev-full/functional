Problem: There are currently 26.2 million programmers planning, designing, developing, testing and maintaining hundreds of millions software applications throughout the world. These programmers help power the world we live in, but if you were to ask every single one of them, what is the most annoying frustrating, and irritating thing about programming, a lot of them will tell you when they have to solve a problem, have a deadline and just can't solve it.

The way the internet is structured today there are a few options. An angry and irritated programmer can go on StackOverflow, Reddit, various forums throughout the internet, Chegg or any other paid answer website. The problem with these solutions is that they have a lot of hype but often fail to live up to expectations. Every programmer knows when you go and ask a question on the internet there is an 80% chance you will get insulted, berated, have your question locked, flagged, have to deal with a mod on a power trip, and finally have your question thread be used as a joke thread. Programmers who do not want to deal with this typically try to go for paid solutions but even then they don't really seem to hit the spot. They are often clunky, too expensive, and just not that good.

Goal: RubberDuckyAnswers's goal is to be the affordable, secret swiss army knife every programmer has in their back pocket. We aim to usher a new chapter in the programming world - one where nobody has to deal with mods on a power trip, jokes on our serious questions, or super expensive solutions on clunky websites. We hope the software development community joins us as we work over the next few months to fully build out this platform.
